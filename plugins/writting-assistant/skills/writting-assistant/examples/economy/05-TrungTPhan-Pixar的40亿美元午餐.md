# Pixar 的 $4B+ 午餐

> 来源：@TrungTPhan
> 链接：https://x.com/TrungTPhan/status/1760140212884172859

---

The most consequential lunch in Hollywood history took place in 1994.

Pixar had one film under its belt — Toy Story, set for release in 1995 — but the studio's future was far from guaranteed.

During a now-legendary lunch, four Pixar brainiacs — Andrew Stanton, John Lasseter, Pete Docter, and Joe Ranft — brainstormed ideas for future films.

From that single meal came the concepts for:
- A Bug's Life (1998)
- Monsters Inc. (2001)
- Finding Nemo (2003)
- Wall-E (2008)

Those 4 films — plus their sequels Monsters University, Finding Dory, and Incredibles 2 (Wall-E's sequel is in development) — have grossed >$4B at the box office.

The Pixar origin story is wild:
- George Lucas founded the graphics division in 1979.
- Steve Jobs bought it from Lucas in 1986 for ~$5M.
- Jobs invested another ~$50M to keep it afloat.
- Pixar IPO'd in 1995 at a ~$1.5B valuation (right before Toy Story dropped).
- Disney acquired Pixar in 2006 for $7.4B.

The lesson: a small group of talented people — given creative freedom — can produce an absurd amount of value.
